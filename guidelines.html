Rules and Regulations

1.0 Eligibility

1.1 The contest is open to legitimate undergraduate students of school year 2019-2020 from all colleges and universities in Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. Graduates may still submit entries provided that the entry was made during their undergraduate years.

1.2 Everyone involved in the production of the film must be undergraduate students at the time the film was made.

1.3 The director, screenwriter, director of photography, and editor must be all legitimate undergraduate students at the time the film was made.

1.4 Participants must indicate if they have incurred help or funding from professional production houses for the making of their film. Participants must specify the name of the production house and what specific work was done for the video.

1.5 Applicants may submit a copy of their entry in any of the following categories:

  1. Feature
  2. Documentary
  3. Music Video
  4. Experimental
  5. Feature SHS
  6. Animation

1.6 The language of the film is open to any dialect. All entries must have English subtitles.

1.7 A participant may submit more than one entry. However, their entries must only be submitted to one category.

1.8 Entries for Feature, Feature SHS, Documentary, Music Video, and Experimental category must not exceed 20 minutes (including opening and closing credits). For the Animation category, the running time must be 3-10 minutes only (including opening and closing credits).

1.9 Entries must be in MP4 format with a minimum quality of 720p. Other formats like Mini DVs, V8, NTSC AVI, and DVD format will not be accepted.

2.0 Eligibility (For SHS)

2.1 This division is open to current Senior High School students (Grade 11-12) of school year 2019-2020 from all schools in Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. Graduates may still be take part given that their film was made during their time in Senior High School.

2.2 Everyone involved in the production of the film must be High School students at the time the film was made.

2.3 Participants must indicate if they have incurred help or funding from professional production houses for the making of their film. Participants must specify the name of the production house and what specific work was done for the video.

2.4 The language of the film is open to any dialect. All entries must have English subtitles.

2.5 A participant may submit more than one entry. However, their entries must only be submitted to one category.

2.6 All entries must not exceed 20 minutes (including opening and closing credits).

2.7 Entries must be in MP4 format with a minimum quality of 720p. Other formats like Mini DVs, V8, NTSC AVI, and DVD format will not be accepted.

3.0 Film Categories

3.1 Music videos must contain an original song from the filmmaker. Otherwise, filmmaker must acquire necessary permission from the artist to use the song in the music video.

3.2 Experimental catgeory is exclusively for films that are non-narrative and non-documentary in nature.

3.3 The guidelines for the Animation category are as follows:

  1. 3.3.1 The judging criteria will be based from the Principles of Animation set out by Frank Thomas and Ollie Johnstone.
  2. 3.3.2 Voices and lines used for dubbing must be originally sourced by the creators. Otherwise, they must acquire necessary permission from the voice artist to use it. A proof of permission must be presented together with the submission of requirements.
  3. 3.3.3 There will be strict implementation of character and graphics copyright. Otherwise, creator must acquire necessary permission from the original animator to use specific graphics in the video.

4.0 Grounds for Disqualification

4.1 Entries that do not have complete set of requirements will be disqualified.

4.2 Films that have been previously awarded in major film competition in the national and international level will be disqualified. However, if an entry has been submitted to another competition but has not won an award at the time of submission in IUF 2020, the entry is still qualified.

4.3 Entries that run over 20 minutes (including opening and closing credits) will be disqualified.

4.4 Entries that contain pornographic elements such as nudity and explicit depiction of sexual activity will be disqualified.

4.5 Entries that do not rightfully acclaim third-party materials that contributed in the making of the film will be disqualified. This includes but is not limited to music, logos, graphics copyrighted materials, and intellectual properties. Thus, we expect participants to acquire necessary permission for any borrowed materials.

4.6 Entries that are submitted past the deadline will not be entertained.
