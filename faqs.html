Frequently Asked Questions

> Who can submit an entry?
The competition is open to legitimate undergraduates and senior highschool students during the academic year 2019-2020 from all schools nationwide. Graduates may also submit an entry provided that the film was made during their undergraduate years.
> How do I submit an entry?
You can submit your entry either manually or through an online submission. A confirmation email will be sent to you after your submission. The procedure can be found in our competition primer at: tinyurl.com/IUF2020Competition.
> What are the categories I can join in IUF?
For the college division, students may submit an entry in any of the following categories: Feature, Documentary, Music Video, Experimental and Animation. Senior High School students may only submit entries in the Feature (SHS) category.
> When is the deadline of the competition?
The deadline for the submission of entries is on May 18, 2020.

> What are the requirements for entries?
All entries for Feature, Feature SHS, Documentary, Music Video and Experimental category should not exceed 20 minutes. Whereas the entries for the Animation category must have a running time of 3-10 minutes only. The opening and closing credits will be counted in the overall duration of each entry. It should be in MP4 format ONLY and have english subtitles.
> Can I submit more than one entry?
Yes, you may submit more than one entry in one or multiple categories. The application fee will apply per entry. However, the same film entry cannot be submitted to multiple categories.
> What are the terms of disqualification for the competition?
Entries that have been awarded in any major film competition will be disqualified. In addition, films that exceed the time-limit or contain any sexual depictions will not be eligible to join the competition.
> What will the winners get?
The winners will receive a certificate, cash prize and tokens from our major sponsors.
> Can I get help from professionals?
Yes. However, you must specify the name of the professional or production house and what specific work was done for the film.
